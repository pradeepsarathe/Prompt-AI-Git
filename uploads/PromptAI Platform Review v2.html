1 · Persona walkthroughs

Four users, four very different experiences

👋 First-time visitor — lands from a shared link

  • Works: instant content paint (one cached fetch + SSR fallback), clean Google-News-familiar layout, "Today in 60 seconds" gives immediate orientation, privacy/terms exist, no popups or cookie walls.
  • Confusing: nothing explains what PromptAI is above the fold — the value prop ("AI briefing + prompts + learning, free") lives in the About panel. A one-line tagline under "Your briefing" would fix it.
  • Trust gap: no social proof anywhere — no subscriber count, no testimonial, no "as seen in". The subscribe popover asks for an email before showing a sample. Link the latest /issue/<date> as "Read a sample briefing →" in every subscribe surface.
  • Conversion blocker: the Subscribe CTA explains cadence ("every Tuesday") but not differentiation — why this over TLDR? The AI summary + prompt-of-the-week is the answer; say it.

🔁 Returning reader — visits 2–3× a week

  • Works: URL hash navigation means bookmarkable tabs; localStorage cache makes repeat loads instant; "recently read" + saved list give continuity; dark theme; PWA installable.
  • Friction: no "what's new since your last visit" cue — every visit looks the same shape. A subtle "12 new stories since Tuesday" marker would create a reason to scan.
  • Missing habit loop: nothing pulls them back — no push notification for the daily summary, no email-me-daily option, no streak. The product is pull-only.

⚡ Power user — researcher / developer, wants depth

  • Works: topic chips, source labels, archive with category filters, RSS feed for their own reader, keyboard-accessible cards, paper explainers on arXiv items.
  • Hits ceilings fast: search has no operators, no date ranges, no source filter, no autocomplete; archive search scans one KV blob; no way to follow a topic ("agents") and see only that; no export of saved items.
  • Underused asset: the archive's "kept forever" papers corpus is a real moat for this audience — but it's only reachable through basic chips.

💌 Subscriber — gets the Tuesday digest

  • Works: double opt-in, one-click unsubscribe, signed links, plain-text alternative, issues archived as web pages — deliverability craft is genuinely above average.
  • Value gap: one email a week with no personalization. Signed-in subscribers who saved 5 agent stories get the same digest as everyone. The "3 stories you saved this week" block exists in code — surface it.
  • No premium path: deliberate (no-paywall promise) — fine, but a voluntary supporter tier ("keep PromptAI independent — $3/mo") is compatible with the promise and tests willingness to pay.

2 · Product strategy

Vision, differentiation, and the one strategic question

The platform now has three genuine differentiators versus newsletter-first competitors: (1) a live, always-fresh briefing site rather than a once-a-day email; (2) an AI layer that summarizes and explains rather than just linking; (3) a free, open, no-paywall stance with real privacy hygiene. The brand name adds a fourth waiting to be claimed: prompts.

The strategic question is focus. PromptAI currently spreads across six surfaces (news, research, deep dives, tools, prompts, learning). Each is decent; none is the best available anywhere. Top-tier outcomes come from being the definitive place for one thing first. The strongest candidates, in order:

  1. The prompt library — brand-aligned, an enormous durable search market, every entry is ownable content, and nobody owns "tested, honest prompt reviews" the way this site's tone could.
  2. "Research, explained" — the paper explainer is the most distinctive feature on the site; one hand-polished explainer per day with its own URL would compound.

Recommendation: keep the briefing as the engagement engine, but make prompts the growth engine. Every other section feeds the funnel.

5 · Search & discovery

Current: client-side substring match over loaded feed + archive blob; cross-page handoff works; no autocomplete, ranking, typo tolerance, or operators. Trending = raw read counts. Recommendations = none beyond "recently read".

  • Near-term (KV-compatible): prefix-match autocomplete over titles + topics; weight matches by recency × read-count; add source:/topic: operators — all client-side over /api/feeds + archive.
  • Right answer (with D1): SQLite FTS5 over the full archive — instant full-text search over every item ever, with date/source facets. This single migration upgrades search, archive, counters and topic pages at once.
  • Recommendation v1 (cheap, no ML): users already save/like; compute top topics from their lists, add a "For you" rail filtered to those topics. Netflix-grade is not needed; visible personalization is.

8 · Accessibility

Now solid: keyboard-reachable cards (Enter/Space), focus trap + restore in modal and sheet, aria-current on tabs, aria-live toast, reduced-motion, color-scheme meta, focus-visible styles.

  • Verify contrast in non-default themes — pill colors (e.g. amber on tinted backgrounds) and --text-3 on --bg-subtle hover states; automate with a Lighthouse CI pass per theme.
  • SSR block for screen readers: ensure #ssr-home is removed from the accessibility tree once hydrated (use hidden, not just visual hiding) or content is announced twice.
  • Translate widget: Google Translate injects DOM that can confuse SRs; confirm lang attribute updates when translation activates.
  • Skip link: none — "skip to content" before the topbar is a 5-line add.

10 · Growth & monetization

  • Funnel today: visit → (maybe) subscribe popover → double opt-in → weekly digest. No activation milestone, no referral, no community. Conversion instrumentation: none beyond pageviews.
  • Free-tier improvements: sample-issue link on every subscribe surface; subscriber count once proud of it; daily-vs-weekly frequency choice; "forwarded this? subscribe" footer (in code — verify live); prompt-of-the-week as the digest hook.
  • Voluntary supporter tier (no-paywall-compatible): $3/mo "keep it independent" — supporters get name in colophon + early features. Tests willingness-to-pay with zero content lockout.
  • Sponsorship (primary revenue): machinery exists (SPONSOR_HTML). Gate: audience numbers. Don't sell before ~1k engaged subscribers; do prepare a one-page media kit now.
  • Referral: newsletter-standard "share link, unlock the prompt vault" — the prompt library doubles as the referral reward inventory.
  • Enterprise/API (later): the aggregated feed + summaries as a JSON API or MCP server for teams; "briefing for your company Slack" is a real wedge.

15 · The brutally honest verdict

Can PromptAI realistically become a top-tier AI knowledge platform? As a global top-5 AI briefing competing head-on with TLDR's distribution or Perplexity's product budget — no, not on current trajectory, and that's the wrong target anyway. As the definitive free AI briefing + prompt library for its niche (independent-minded readers, India-first timing, privacy-respecting, genuinely free) — yes, realistically, within 12–18 months. The engineering is no longer the constraint; it's now meaningfully better than several larger competitors' web products.

What's actually required, in order:

  1. Stop building features for ~90 days. The punch list is done. Every hour now goes to content and distribution — the platform is a race car parked in a garage.
  2. Publish original content on a cadence: 3 explainers + 5 new prompts per week, each with its own URL. ~150 ownable pages in 6 months is the difference between ranking and not existing.
  3. Automate distribution: auto-post every issue, submit everywhere once, and personally share the best explainer weekly. Backlinks decide this category.
  4. Instrument the funnel, then sell the sponsor slot when you can show open rates and subscriber counts — not before.
  5. Pick the identity: "PromptAI — the AI briefing with prompts that actually work." Say it on the homepage, in the digest, in the OG description. Every great niche product is a sentence first.

The uncomfortable truth: the hardest remaining work isn't technical, and no further code review will move the needle. The next assessment that matters is Google Search Console's.
